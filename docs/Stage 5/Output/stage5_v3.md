## The gradient system (author-specified, now the law of the instrument)
Three gradients applied everywhere, replacing the stock colormaps:
**OPENNESS** `#002D72` navy (enclosed) → `#573894` → `#81459E` → `#A0E5D9` mint (open) · **PRIVACY** `#FFF3E6` cream (public) → `#722F37` wine (private) · **REACHABILITY** `#3B3432` umber (hardest) → `#44573F` moss → `#EDF1E6` sage (easiest). **Aesthetic assessment, as requested:** the three families read as cool (openness), warm (privacy), earthen (reachability) — chromatically distinct yet united by one perceptual rule: *light = open / public / reachable; dark = enclosed / private / withdrawn*. That single rule is what makes the composition integrated rather than merely coordinated, and the cream paper ground (#FBF8F2) ties the warm family to the page. One reasoned adjustment (explicitly invited): the author's reach ramp ran moss→umber — two dark ends, which cannot read as a gradient; a light sage head was added so the ramp has luminance travel, keeping both author colours in-ramp and preserving the shared rule. The divergence accent moved to **vermilion `#E8590C`** — outside all three ramps, so it can never be mistaken for data. Δ jump labels take a neutral amber for the same reason.

## Structural changes, item by item
**Reachability across storeys** — L1+L2 now side by side on one shared scale with a single colorbar, exactly mirroring the privacy pair. **Justified graph** — the ring/fill dual coding is retired for **half-moon nodes**: left half = openness, right half = privacy — the two core qualities on one simple shape (the author's "apply the two colormaps everywhere" made literal). Carrier is now text on a ground line, no box. The legend moved clear of the drawing and became comprehensive, including the Δ numbers: *Δn = the felt jump — the change in the 0–100 quality score as you cross that threshold (Δ0 seamless · ~20 eased · 45+ a dramatic reveal or compression)*. **Genotype** — encodings kept (radius = integration with the most integrated nearest the centre; sectors = privacy zones with gaps), restyled after the author's radial reference: tapered **petals whose width = floor area**, colour = the wine privacy gradient, rim name-bubbles on dotted spokes, dotted radial guides, cream ground. **Serial vision** — the middle curve is now explained on the sheet itself: it is *the same 0–100 score as the quality lens, sampled along the walk* — the plans show the whole field; the curve shows the sequence a person actually walks. Probes follow the **experiential sequence** the author specified: entrance · middle of each room · **stair bottom** (looking around below) · **stair top** (the emerging view, arriving above — same footprint point, computed against the upper storey's geometry: Cullen's existing/emerging pair, computed) · through to the destination's threshold. The room band now rides the openness gradient. **Board B** — ¾-circle rosettes (the open quadrant holds the reading guides), room **name at the centre** (≤10 characters, auto-shortened), register-coloured stems, vermilion divergence ring, and a master legend stating **both poles of every axis** ("open — rim: the most open room · centre: the most enclosed", etc.). The 3D-perspective layer remains assessed-and-skipped (tool weight); the isovist snapshot is the rough image of the space at zero added tooling.

## v3.2 — petal geometry hardened (fundamental)
A wide-room distortion surfaced on FZK's Galerie (158° area chunk): the petal's tip was closed by a straight chord, which — across a wide angle — cuts back near the chart's centre and self-intersects the fill into a twisted ribbon. Fixed in `_petal` itself, for any future floorplan: the tip is now a SAMPLED ARC (valid at any angular width), the petal glyph's half-width is capped at 11° (the room's true area share remains visible as its allocated chunk/sector shading), and a minimum radial length guarantees maximally-integrated rooms render as a visible nub rather than a hairline.
